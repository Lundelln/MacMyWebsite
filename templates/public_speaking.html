New city. New job. New website. Who dis?

I am lingering with the feeling of how scary it is to publish something you have written like this.

0. My minimum viable product (MVP).

My website is far from finished. My thoughts are far from polished. That said, I will rather start now, than waiting for too long and maybe never start. I better just jump into it! With this first post, I will share my personal reasons for why I am creating a website and why do it through a platform like this.

1. Creating my website from scratch - a great way to learn new technologies.

I want a side project that encourages me to play around with technologies that I have had to learn during my education, and now my work. Instead of creating a website with automated tools like Wix, Squarespace, and Wordpress, I want a playground where I can create something with new technologies and frameworks.

The now first version of the website is developed with Python and the web framework Flask, hosted by DigitalOcean. I learned how to use these tools through a part-time job and wanted to dive deeper into these technologies.

Currently, I am doing a migration over to the .NET world and hosting with Microsoft Azure. A move motivated by my new job in Avanade. I already see a huge benefit of this project - accelerating my learning of the new technology and working environments.

2. Where is the path to thought leadership?

During the last few years, I have had the pleasure of doing a podcast and talks about technology, leadership and time management. This has given me a lot of personal value and opportunities that I definitely did not see coming.

I hope that this new project of writing and sharing will enable me to learn more and welcome new opportunities.

3. Self-reflection.

Editing audio files with my own voice and editing text for academia has given me great insight into where I am today and how much I have learned since I started on my masters. I think that projects like these can easily be forgotten when I now start working. I hope that this project will force me to continue on the same path on which I have started.

Tomorrow I will present my website and thoughts about this subject in front of my colleagues. It will be very interesting to see the response and get more feedback on what I am doing.
